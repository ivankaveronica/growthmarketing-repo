I had a great catch up with my homie Nigel Stevens this AM regarding the state of B2B marketing, content, SEO/GEO, navigating the chaos of working with tech companies, etc. What we both landed on about the current environment is there's "NO CHILL" anymore. Working with clients is intense. Questions and concerns are coming through fast and furiously. Fridays at 2PM. Mondays at 9AM. It doesn't matter. Slack and email is buzzing nonstop. New requests are popping up left and right. As an in-house marketer, life in big tech is chaotic. It's constant meetings and fire drills. Everything is "urgent" because the board asked for it. That translates into anxiety which flows down to teams and consultants. Everyone feels like they are fighting for survival. Cuts are happening like crazy. Budgets are getting scrutinized. In-house marketers are on edge. On the consulting side, sometimes you win and sometimes you lose. 99% of my client churn is due to a new CMO or VP coming in and initiating a regime change. Even if you've been killing it -- doesn't matter. Past performance is meaningless in these scenarios. You're a line item on the marketing budget, and the new CMO doesn't think you're worth it. On the flipside, I've also been the beneficiary of a CMO wanting to bring me along for their new adventure. Wil Reynolds wrote a great medium post about this. Going back to the main point about NO CHILL. Things were actually easier before AI in terms of expectations. You had a marketing playbook. You ran it, and it worked. Drive hoardes of top of funnel. Rank track your keywords. Run Google PPC campaigns. Publish whitepapers and promote webinars. Run gated content. Run A|B tests. Let the sales machine do its thing. Report the numbers. Learn how to talk and sound smart. It works. Before COVID it was even easier. The golden era of SaaS was 2011 - 2020. Look at the stock market. It was the golden decade for the QQQ / NASDAQ. As an employee, you jump on a big company bandwagon. Ride the wave. Watch your stock based comp get fully vested and fly to the moon. Now, we're at a breaking point. It's like playing black jack and hitting when you already have a 19. Everything is oversaturated. Everything is noisy. Everything is harder. Engagement is down. Algorithms run the world. That's just the way it's going to be. And that's the post.
