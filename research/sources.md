## Annotations

### 1. Sam Oh — Ahrefs
Sam Oh is the clearest proof that YouTube works for B2B SaaS. He built Ahrefs' channel from zero to 550K+ subscribers by doing one thing most B2B brands avoid: showing up consistently on camera, every week, for years. His strategy is search-first — every video targets a keyword his ICP is actively looking for, with the product embedded naturally into the solution. The result: 40,000 customers directly attributed to YouTube content. Ahrefs' channel is the benchmark every B2B SaaS team references when they ask "what does good look like?"

### 2. Ross Simmonds — Foundation Marketing
Ross coined "Create Once, Distribute Forever" — a framework that reframes YouTube not as a standalone channel but as the anchor of a full content distribution system. His agency Foundation works with B2B SaaS brands like Canva, Snowflake, and Bitly, and his own research found that YouTube videos outrank entire SEO teams across 1,700+ keywords. What makes him unique is that he also built Distribution.ai — an AI tool that automates content distribution — meaning he's not just theorizing about scale, he's building infrastructure for it.

### 3. Adam Robinson — RB2B
Adam Robinson is the most extreme case study in this list. He replaced his entire sales team with founder-led content on LinkedIn and YouTube, and took RB2B from $0 to $1M ARR in just 16 weeks — faster than any of his previous companies. His YouTube strategy is raw and documentary-style: he shares real revenue numbers, real failures, and real decisions in real time. The result is that prospects arrive at sales calls already trusting him, having watched hours of his content. He proves that YouTube doesn't need to be polished to generate pipeline — it needs to be real.

### 4. Samu Kovacs — KS Media
Samu Kovacs is the only person in the world who operates a B2B business exclusively around YouTube strategy. While others teach it as one channel among many, his entire agency KS Media exists to help B2B companies turn YouTube into a revenue channel — not a brand awareness play. His client roster includes Sam Dunning's Breaking B2B and Adam Robinson's RB2B. His framework focuses on three things: ranking for keywords your ICP searches, staying top-of-mind between touchpoints, and warming up leads before they ever get on a call. With 40+ active clients, his approach is the most battle-tested B2B YouTube playbook available.

### 5. Sam Dunning — Breaking B2B
Sam Dunning doesn't just teach B2B YouTube strategy — he used it to grow his own agency from $50K to $185K per month in revenue within 22 months. His channel Breaking B2B consistently ranks at the top of YouTube search for terms like "B2B SEO" and "SaaS marketing" — meaning his ideal clients find him before he ever reaches out to them. He reports that YouTube videos are now mentioned in nearly every sales call his team takes, because prospects have already spent hours watching his content before the first conversation. His is the most transparent and verifiable case study of YouTube-as-growth-engine in the B2B agency space.

### 6. Gaetano DiNardi — ex-Nextiva
Gaetano DiNardi is the practitioner who figured out how to make YouTube and SEO work together, not separately. While at Nextiva, he pushed his team to go all-in on YouTube SEO because "nobody in the industry was doing video well" — and the result was that their videos and blog posts began double-occupying Google search results simultaneously, getting two placements where competitors got one. He helped Nextiva scale from $70M to over $200M in revenue, with content as the primary inbound engine. He now operates as a growth advisor and shares tactical breakdowns on LinkedIn and through podcast appearances.

### 7. Chris Savage — Wistia
Chris Savage built Wistia to 100,000+ customers without hiring a single salesperson — purely through content. As the CEO of a B2B video hosting SaaS, he's in a unique position: his company lives or dies by proving that video works for B2B, so everything Wistia publishes is a direct demonstration of their own strategy. His "Show Business" approach treats B2B content like original programming — producing documentary series and branded shows rather than just tutorial videos. Wistia's annual State of Video report is the most cited industry research on B2B video trends, and Chris actively publishes the data showing how AI is changing video production at scale.

### 8. Brendan Hufford — Growth Sprints
Brendan Hufford is included specifically as a critical voice. While most experts in this list show what great B2B YouTube looks like, Brendan documents what bad looks like — and why it's so common. His observation: most SaaS companies invest heavily in video production but spend zero time on titles, thumbnails, or platform optimization, effectively "dumping" content onto YouTube rather than building a strategy. This counter-perspective is valuable because it explains why the gap exists between B2B companies that win on YouTube and the majority that don't. His Content IP framework and Growth Sprints practice serve B2B SaaS companies scaling from $10M to $100M ARR.

### 9. Udi Ledergor — ex-Gong
Udi Ledergor built one of the most recognized brand voices in B2B SaaS history. As CMO of Gong, he took the company from a new startup to a multi-billion dollar valuation — not through outbound or paid acquisition, but through content that people actually wanted to consume. Gong's YouTube channel became a benchmark for human-centric B2B content: data-driven insights from real sales calls, delivered in a voice that felt more like a media company than a software vendor. His "95/5 rule" — creating content for the 95% of the market not actively buying, not just the 5% in-market — is one of the most practical frameworks for thinking about YouTube's role in long-term B2B pipeline.

### 10. Kieran Flanagan — HubSpot
Kieran Flanagan is the bridge between B2B YouTube strategy and the future of content production. As SVP Marketing at HubSpot, he quadrupled marketing demand by building HubSpot's media operation — including acquiring The Hustle — and co-hosting "Marketing Against the Grain," one of the top B2B marketing podcasts on YouTube. What makes him particularly relevant to this research: he has publicly shared how he uses AI tools to build content production systems that one person can run, predicting that B2B brands without in-house creators will increasingly turn to AI-generated content to maintain a YouTube presence. He represents where B2B YouTube strategy is heading, not just where it's been.
