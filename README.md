# YouTube Content Strategy for B2B SaaS — Research Project

## Topic
YouTube Content Strategy for B2B SaaS

## Why I Chose This Topic
My background is in YouTube content production for a business and finance media channel — which means I've spent significant time understanding how the algorithm works, how to optimize for search, and what makes a video actually get found versus get buried. That hands-on experience made this topic feel immediately legible to me: the mechanics of YouTube SEO that B2B SaaS companies rely on aren't fundamentally different from what any serious YouTube channel has to master.

What drew me deeper into this specific angle was realizing how underutilized YouTube is in B2B contexts. Most SaaS companies either ignore it entirely or treat it as a dumping ground for product demos and webinar recordings — with no attention to titles, thumbnails, or search intent. Yet the companies that do it well, like Ahrefs and Gong, have built entire customer acquisition engines on the back of it.

I was also genuinely curious about how AI is starting to change content production workflows at scale — something I'd been observing in the creator space and wanted to understand better in a B2B context. That curiosity shaped how I selected experts: I didn't just look for people who talk about YouTube strategy, I looked for people actively using it to generate pipeline, and increasingly, using AI to do it more efficiently.

## What I Found
The clearest pattern across all 10 experts: the B2B companies winning on YouTube are not treating it as a brand awareness channel. They're treating it as a search engine — one that happens to also build trust over time.

Every practitioner in this list, regardless of company size or industry, converges on the same core principle: start with what your ICP is actively searching for, build content around that intent, and let the algorithm work in your favor. This is YouTube SEO applied to B2B — and it compounds in a way that paid ads never can.

Several findings surprised me:

**YouTube and Google SEO are more connected than most teams realize.** Gaetano DiNardi's experience at Nextiva demonstrated this concretely: optimized YouTube videos can appear in Google search results simultaneously with blog posts, effectively doubling a company's SERP real estate with the same content investment. This makes YouTube a multiplier, not just an additional channel.

**The sales cycle shortens significantly when prospects have watched your content.** Sam Dunning, Samu Kovacs, and Adam Robinson all independently reported the same phenomenon: leads who arrive on sales calls having watched YouTube content close faster and ask fewer basic questions. YouTube functions as a pre-qualification and trust-building layer that no cold outreach can replicate.

**Founder-led content outperforms brand content consistently.** Adam Robinson replaced his entire sales team with personal content. Kieran Flanagan co-hosts a podcast as an individual, not a HubSpot property. The experts who saw the strongest results were those who put a real person — not a brand — at the center of their YouTube strategy.

**AI is beginning to reshape the production layer, not the strategy layer.** Tools like those Ross Simmonds built at Distribution.ai, and the systems Kieran Flanagan has publicly shared, are helping teams produce and distribute content faster — but the underlying strategy remains human. The insight still has to come from someone who understands the customer. AI handles the execution; judgment still determines what gets made.

**A note on AEO (Answer Engine Optimization):** During this research, I encountered early signals that B2B content teams are beginning to optimize not just for YouTube and Google search, but for AI-powered answer engines like ChatGPT and Perplexity. This is an emerging layer on top of traditional SEO — and given how quickly AI search is growing, it's likely to become a significant consideration for B2B YouTube strategy in the next 12–18 months. It's not yet a standard practice among the experts in this list, but it's a direction worth tracking.
